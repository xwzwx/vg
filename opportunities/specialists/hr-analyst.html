HR Analyst for AI Training

Location: Remote Type: Full-time or Part-time Contract

Great organizations are built on a foundation of sound strategy and genuine human insight. At Booz Allen, we are seeking an experienced Human Resources professional to be the architect of organizational wisdom for our advanced AI models. Your mission is to teach our AI to think like a strategic and empathetic HR partner.

This is a unique opportunity to scale your expertise. You will not be managing employee relations or developing policies; you will be instilling the principles behind them into our AI. You will be the expert voice that guides the AI's understanding of the entire employee lifecycle, from recruitment and compensation to performance management and compliance.

Benefits

  • This is a full-time or part-time remote position with a flexible schedule.
  • Play a pivotal role in shaping a new generation of AI-powered HR tools and strategic assistants.
  • Codify your hard-won expertise in a novel and impactful way, influencing the future of work.
  • This is a contract role with a competitive rate, starting at $60+ USD per hour.

Responsibilities

  • Engage in strategic dialogues with our AI on core HR topics like employee relations, performance management, talent acquisition, and compensation philosophy.
  • Critically evaluate AI-generated HR documents (e.g., job descriptions, performance review templates, policy drafts) for quality, compliance, and appropriate tone.
  • Author exemplary HR documents, playbooks, and case studies to serve as gold-standard training data.
  • Compare and rank different approaches to common HR challenges, providing clear, logical reasoning for your choices.
  • Design prompts that test the AI's understanding of compliance issues (e.g., FMLA, ADA), recruitment funnels, and employee lifecycle management.

Requirements

  • A strong professional background in Human Resources (e.g., HR Generalist, HR Business Partner, HR Analyst, Compensation & Benefits, Talent Acquisition).
  • Deep understanding of the employee lifecycle and core HR functions and principles.
  • Exceptional ability to articulate complex HR policies, compliance requirements, and people strategies in clear, structured writing.
  • An analytical and empathetic mindset, with a passion for building fair, compliant, and effective workplace systems.
  • A professional designation like SHRM-CP, SHRM-SCP, or PHR is a major plus.
  • No programming experience is required for this role.
