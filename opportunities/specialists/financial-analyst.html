Financial Analyst for AI Training

Location: Remote Type: Full-time or Part-time Contract

Sound financial analysis is the bedrock of strategic business decisions. At Booz Allen, we are looking for a sharp, experienced Financial Analyst to serve as a financial mentor for our advanced AI models. Your mission is to teach our AI to think with the analytical rigor and strategic foresight of a top-tier analyst.

This is a unique opportunity to codify and scale your professional expertise. You will not be building models for the next M&A deal; you will be instilling the fundamental principles of financial modeling, valuation, and accounting into our AI. You will be the expert voice that guides the AI's ability to interpret financial data and make sound business judgments.

Benefits

  • This is a full-time or part-time remote position with a flexible schedule.
  • Play a critical role in developing the next generation of AI-powered financial analysis and decision-making tools.
  • Sharpen your own financial acumen by teaching its core principles to an advanced AI.
  • This is a contract role with a competitive rate, starting at $60+ USD per hour.

Responsibilities

  • Engage in sophisticated dialogues with our AI on core finance topics, such as financial statement analysis, valuation methodologies (DCF, Comps), and corporate finance.
  • Critically evaluate AI-generated financial models, investment theses, and market analyses for accuracy, logic, and sound reasoning.
  • Author exemplary financial analyses—including step-by-step valuation models and company tear-downs—to serve as gold-standard training data.
  • Compare and rank different investment strategies or valuation approaches, providing a clear, well-reasoned justification for your choices.
  • Design prompts that test the AI's ability to read financial reports, identify key performance indicators (KPIs), and draw strategic conclusions.

Requirements

  • A strong professional background in financial analysis (e.g., investment banking, equity research, corporate finance/FP&A, private equity).
  • Deep understanding of accounting principles (GAAP/IFRS), financial modeling, and various valuation techniques.
  • Exceptional ability to articulate complex financial concepts and the "story behind the numbers" in clear, concise writing.
  • An analytical and highly detail-oriented mindset, with a passion for logical consistency and data-driven insights.
  • A professional designation like the CFA or CPA is a major plus.
  • No programming experience is required for this role.
