AI Research Engineer

Location: Remote Type: Full-time or Part-time Contract

At Velocity Global, we don't just apply existing AI techniques; we are driven to discover what's next. We are seeking a pioneering AI Research Engineer to be our scout on the frontier of artificial intelligence, exploring uncharted territory and turning groundbreaking ideas into reality.

In this role, you will bridge the gap between abstract theory and practical application. You will transform state-of-the-art academic research into tangible prototypes and novel capabilities for our models. If you are driven by fundamental questions and the thrill of building what has never been built before, this is your opportunity to define the future of our technology.

Benefits

  • This is a full-time or part-time remote position with a flexible schedule.
  • Work on the most challenging, open-ended problems in AI, with the freedom to experiment and innovate.
  • See your research have a clear path to impact as successful prototypes are integrated into our core products.
  • This is a contract position with a highly competitive rate, starting at $70+ USD per hour.

Responsibilities

  • Stay at the forefront of AI research by reading academic papers, following top conferences (e.g., NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR), and exploring new open-source projects.
  • Design and conduct rigorous experiments to validate new hypotheses and test novel AI techniques.
  • Develop novel algorithms and model architectures to address fundamental challenges in areas like reasoning, efficiency, and robustness.
  • Build and test proof-of-concept prototypes to demonstrate the feasibility and potential of new research ideas.
  • Collaborate closely with Machine Learning Engineers to help transition successful research into our production pipelines.
  • Document and present research findings to both technical and non-technical audiences within the company.

Requirements

  • 2+ years of experience in a research-focused AI/ML role or an equivalent academic background (e.g., a Master's or PhD in a relevant field).
  • Strong proficiency in Python and deep experience with ML frameworks like PyTorch or TensorFlow.
  • A deep theoretical understanding of modern machine learning, particularly in areas like transformer architectures, reinforcement learning, or generative models.
  • A track record of successfully implementing and experimenting with complex algorithms from research papers.
  • A curious, first-principles thinker who is comfortable with ambiguity and thrives on tackling unsolved problems.
  • Excellent problem-solving skills and a scientific mindset for experimentation and analysis.
